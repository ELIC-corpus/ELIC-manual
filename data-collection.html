ELIC corpus: Procedures and guidelines

Chapter 1 Data collection

Interviews should be conducted by native speakers of the individual variety or a similar variety whenever possible. All portions of the interview should be recorded.

1.1 Recruiting speakers

For this research we are seeking fluent and active speakers of the individual varieties being studied. If possible, you should try to recruit speakers of different ages, both male and female. Our goal is to document the varieties as they are spoken today, so you should not focus solely on the oldest generation, who may be considered to speak the “purest” version of the given variety. Speakers may code-switch during the interview; this is to be expected, and this does not disqualify them from participation.

When inviting individuals to participate in this project, you may say something like the following:

Pozivamo vas na sudjelovanje u projektu Ugroženi jezici u dodiru u Istri i Kvarneru: Višejezični govorni korpus za jezično dokumentiranje i istraživanje/Endangered languages in contact in Istria and Kvarner, Croatia: A multilingual spoken corpus for language documentation and research. Cilj razgovora s Vama je prikupljanje govorne građe u svrhu dokumentiranja, očuvanja i znanstvenog proučavanja ______ (ime jezičnog varijeteta). Razgovor će se voditi vezano za teme iz svakodnevnog života i neka Vaša životna iskustva, a na kraju razgovora ispunit ćemo zajedno kratki demografski upitnik. Intervju će trajati najviše devedesetak minuta. Razgovor će biti anoniman a audiosnimka razgovora će biti pohranjenja u buduću jezičnu arhivu projekta. Ova će se audiosnimka moći koristiti isključivo u znanstvene svrhe a vaše sudjelovanje je dobrovoljno. Ukoliko vas zanima, možete dobiti snimku intervjua.

1.2 Materials needed

You will need the following materials for the interview:

  1. Digital recorder (provided by the principal investigators). Make sure that the recorder is fully charged and that you have the charger with you (or spare batteries, if applicable).
  2. Oral consent form (which you will read and fill out)
  3. GDPR consent, no signature (which you will give to the speaker after they provide their oral consent to participate)
  4. Sociolinguistic interview guide (which you will use to conduct the interview)
  5. Demographic questionnaire (which you will fill out with the participant at the end of the interview and later enter the answers into the Google form)

These documents are available in the folders labeled “Consent documents” and “Data collection documents” in the shared OneDrive folder.

1.3 Before the interview

  • Read all of the documents carefully, especially the sociolinguistic interview guide, so that the interview will go smoothly.

  • Familiarize yourself with using the recorder before you conduct the interview. Interviews should be recorded in mono, rather than stereo (which is often the default setting on recorders). The recording format should be: .wav, 44.1 kHz sampling rate, 16-bit resolution. Verify these settings prior to the interview. Practice making some short recordings and setting the recording level appropriately. (Ask one of the principal investigators how to do this if you’re not sure.) Make sure that enough space is available on the memory card. A 90-minute interview recorded with these settings will require approximately 1.5 GB.

  • Determine the code to be used for the speaker (you will fill this in on the oral consent form during the interview). Speaker codes consist of a 3-letter abbreviation for the local dialect used by the speaker, which is typically the same as the location of the recording, followed by a two-digit number. For example, Istro-Venetian speakers from Pula would be identified as PUL01, PUL02, etc. Consult with one of the principle investigators if you are not sure what code to use.

  • When arranging the time and place for the interview, you should inquire about the availability of a quiet location where you will not be interrupted. See 1.4 below.

1.4 During the interview

  • Choose a quiet location where you can be alone with the participant (if possible). You should avoid recording in a kitchen or outdoors (e.g., on a terrace or balcony). Generally a living room or other space that has curtains, rugs, and upholstered furniture like couches and armchairs is best. If possible, close the windows and turn off all possible sources of noise (air-conditioners, fans, television, telephones). Don’t forget to turn off your own mobile phone!

  • Set up the recorder and position the microphone close to the speaker. Place it on a stable surface – ideally, on a table between you and the participant – and caution the participant not to bump or hit the table during the interview. If possible, remove anything from the table that might make noise (e.g., glasses, cups, ashtrays, etc.). Make a test recording to check the equipment and recording level.

  • When everything is ready, start recording. If possible, do not turn off the recorder until the very end of the interview, after the demographic questionnaire. If you are briefly interrupted, you should leave the recorder on; we can delete these portions of the recording later.

  • State your name, the date, and the name of the city/town/village where the interview is being conducted.

  • Read the oral consent script and obtain the participant’s consent. Fill out the requested information on the oral consent form.

  • Give the participant a copy of the GDPR no signature consent document for their records.

  • Conduct the interview (approximately 1 hour in length). See the sociolinguistic interview guide for suggested questions. You should follow the order of sections in this guide, but you do not have to include every section or every question. You may follow the lead of the participant and spend more time on topics that are of interest to them, but try to include several different topics, and do not focus entirely on the past – be sure to include topics/questions that deal with their current activities and interests. The only obligatory topic is Language (#11), so reserve some time for this at the end of the interview.

  • Complete the demographic questionnaire orally with the participant. Fill in the answers on your copy of the questionnaire.

  • Stop the recording.

  • Thank the participant for their time and ask again if they would like to receive a copy of the interview recording. If so, please send it to them as soon as possible by e-mail, or provide it on a CD or USB memory stick. (You will be reimbursed for any costs for doing this.)

1.5 After the interview

  • Transfer the recording to your computer and name the file, following the conventions in 2.1.

  • Upload the file to the folder “Upload original interview recordings here” in the shared OneDrive folder for the project and notify Keith Langston.

  • Upload a scan of the completed Oral consent form to the folder “Upload consent documents here”

  • Fill out the Google form for the demographic questionnaire.
